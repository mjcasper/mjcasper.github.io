Exam 3 Study Guide

Logistics

  • Date: Thursday, NovemberÂ 21,Â 2019

  • Time: 8:45pm-10:15pm ET - (it is a 1.5 hour exam)

  • Location: Your exam location is posted on UBlearns.

  • What to Bring: You should bring multiple pens / pencils, your approved calculator, and a photo ID.

    • Calculator: You are allowed to use only the Texas Instruments TI-30XIIS calculator on the exam. All other calculators are prohibited. See the Calculator and Reference Policy in the syllabus for more details.

    • Photo ID: You must bring a photo ID with you to your test site (either a UB student ID or a state-issued ID like a driver's licencse).

  • What Not to Bring: Do not bring any bags, books, notes, formula sheets, or other loose items.

    • No Formula Sheets: You are not allowed any outside references (e.g., cheat sheets, formula sheets, note cards, or textbooks). See the Calculator and Reference Policy in the syllabus for more details.

Material

  • Topics: Exam 3 will explicitly cover material from Ch.4 - Ch.5. Although you will definitely need to know our various derivative rules, like the Product, Quotient, and Chain Rules.

  • Questions: Exam 3 will consist of approximately 15-20 short-answer questions and 3-5 long-answer questions.

Learning Outcomes Being Tested

Below is the list of potential exam questions for each section covered in lecture.

Section 4.1

Additional Practice 4.1 #29, 31, 35, 43, 47, 49, 53, 73 (textbook)

  1. Given the graph of a function, identify the Absolute and Local Extrema.
  2. Find the critical numbers of \(f(x)\).
    • may require use of the product, quotient, or chain rules.
  3. Use the Closed Interval Method to find the absolute extrema of a continuous function defined on a closed interval.
    • may require use of the product, quotient, or chain rules.

Section 4.2

Additional Practice 4.2 #1, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 15, 37 (textbook)

  1. State the entire hypothesis and conclusion of Rolle's Theorem.
  2. Verify that a function satisfies the hypotheses of Rolle's Theorem.
  3. Find the number(s) \(c\) that satisfy the conclusion of Rolle's Theorem.
  4. Determine which of the hypotheses of Rolle's Theorem fails for a given function and interval.
  5. State the entire hypothesis and conclusion of the Mean Value Theorem.
  6. Verify that a function satisfies the hypotheses of the Mean Value Theorem.
  7. Find the mean (or average) slope of a function on a given interval.
  8. Find the number(s) \(c\) that satisfy the conclusion of the Mean Value Theorem.

Sections 4.3 and 4.5

Additional Practice 4.3 #1, 3, 5, 9, 13, 27, 35, 37, 39 --- 4.5 #1, 3, 5, 11, 45 (textbook)

  • For all of the following problems, you may be given:
    • the derivatives, \(f'(x)\) or \(f''(x)\), already calculated for you.
    • only the function \(f(x)\), and need to calculate the derivatives for yourself.

  1. Find and Classify the critical numbers of a function using:

    • the 1st Derivative Test (relative max, relative min, or neither)
    • the 2nd Derivative Test (relative max, relative min, or inconclusive)

  2. Use \(f'(x)\) to find the following about \(f(x)\):

    • Intervals of increase / decrease.
    • Local extreme points.

  3. Use \(f''(x)\) to find the following about \(f(x)\):

    • Intervals of concave up / concave down.
    • Inflection points.

  4. Given the graph of \(f(x)\):

    • Find the open interval(s) where \(f'(x)\) is positive or negative.
    • Find the open interval(s) where \(f''(x)\) is positive or negative.
    • Find the \(x\)-value(s) where \(f'(x)=0\)
    • Find the \(x\)-value(s) where \(f''(x)=0\)

  5. Sketch the graph of a function by determining the:

    • Intervals of increase / decrease.
    • Local extreme points.
    • Intervals of concave up / concave down.
    • Inflection points.

Section 4.4

Additional Practice 4.4 #9, 11, 13, 15, 17, 19, 21, 25, 27, 35, 47, 53, 57 (textbook)

For L'Hospital's Rule you will need to be able to state exactly where you are using the rule in your work, and the type of Indeterminate Form.

  1. Compute limits for \(0/0\) indeterminate forms.
  2. Compute limits for \(\infty/\infty\) indeterminate forms.
  3. Be able to determine when L'Hospital's Rule is and is not applicable.
  4. Compute limits for \(0\cdot\infty\) indeterminate forms by first rewriting the product as a quotient.
  5. Compute limits for \(\infty-\infty\) indeterminate forms by first rewriting the difference as a product or quotient.
  6. Compute limits for \(0^{0}\), \(\infty^{0}\), \(1^{\infty}\) indeterminate forms using the natural logarithm.

Section 4.7

Additional Practice 4.7 #3, 7, 15, 17, 21, 34, 35, 49, 51, 63 (textbook)

  1. Determine the Objective and Constraint Equations for an optimization word problem.
  2. Solve an optimization problem involving one of the following:
    • perimeter and area of a rectangle
    • cost and perimeter / area of a rectangle
    • surface area and volume of a 3-dimensional box
    • distance between the points on a curve and some other point.
  3. You will need to justify that your answer results in a Maximum or Minimum, using either the First or Second Derivative Test.

Sections 4.9 and 5.4a

Additional Practice 4.9 #1, 3, 7, 9, 13, 21, 23, 27, 33, 45 --- 5.4 #5-11(odd) (textbook)

  1. Calculate the basic form indefinite integrals:

    • \(f(x)=k\)
    • \(f(x)=x^{n}\qquad n\neq-1\)
    • \(f(x)=x^{-1}\)
    • \(f(x)=e^{x}\) or \(b^x\)
    • \(\sin x\), \(\cos x\)
    • \(\sec^{2}x\), \(\csc^{2}x\), \(\sec x \tan x\), \(\csc x \cot x\)
    • \(\frac{1}{1+x^{2}}\) and \(\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}\)

  2. Calculate indefinite integrals involving sums, differences, and constant multiples of the basic forms listed above.

    • You may need to rewrite roots and fractions as powers of \(x\) first.
    • You may need to perform some multiplication or division first before integrating.

  3. Identify the properties of Indefinite Integrals.

  4. Determine the function \(f(x)\) given \(f'(x)\) and a specified value \(f(a)=b\).

  5. Determine the function \(f(x)\) given \(f''(x)\) and specified values \(f(a)=b\) and \(f(c)=d\) or \(f(a)=b\) and \(f'(c)=d\).

Section 5.5

Additional Practice 5.5 #3, 7, 9, 13, 15, 21, 25, 33 (textbook)

  1. Choose an appropriate value for \(u\) in order to solve an indefinite integral using the substitution rule. (Only choosing a value for \(u\) here, not actually finishing the integration).
  2. Calculate an indefinite integral using the substitution rule from start to finish.
