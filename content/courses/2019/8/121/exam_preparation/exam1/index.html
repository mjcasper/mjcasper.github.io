Exam 1 Study Guide

Logistics

  • Date: Thursday, SeptemberÂ 26,Â 2019

  • Time: 8:45pm-10:15pm ET - (it is a 1.5 hour exam)

  • Location: Your exam location is posted on UBlearns.

  • What to Bring: You should bring multiple pens / pencils, your approved calculator, and a photo ID.

    • Calculator: You are allowed to use only the Texas Instruments TI-30XIIS calculator on the exam. All other calculators are prohibited. See the Calculator and Reference Policy in the syllabus for more details.

    • Photo ID: You must bring a photo ID with you to your test site (either a UB student ID or a state-issued ID like a driver's licencse).

  • What Not to Bring: Do not bring any bags, books, notes, formula sheets, or other loose items.

    • No Formula Sheets: You are not allowed any outside references (e.g., cheat sheets, formula sheets, note cards, or textbooks). See the Calculator and Reference Policy in the syllabus for more details.

Material

  • Topics: Exam 1 will explicitly cover material from Ch.0 - Ch.1

  • Questions: Exam 1 will consist of approximately 15-20 short-answer questions and 3-5 long-answer questions

Learning Outcomes Being Tested

Below is the list of potential exam questions for each section covered in lecture.

Section 0.1

Additional Practice Textbook problems: 0.1 #13, 15, 17, 21, 23, 25, 27, 53

  1. Evaluate a function at some given \(x\) value.
  2. Evaluate a piecewise function at some given \(x\) value.
  3. Determine the domain of a function.
  4. Use a graph to evaluate a function at some given \(x\) value.
  5. Sketch the graph of a function by plotting points.

Section 0.2

Additional Practice Textbook problems: 0.2 #1, 3, 9, 9, 31, 33, 35

  1. Sketch the graph of a linear function.
  2. Sketch the graph of a quadratic function.
  3. Sketch the graph of a piecewise function that has pieces involving linear and/or quadratic functions.

Section 0.3

Additional Practice Textbook problems: 0.3 #7, 9, 31, 33

  1. Express the sum or difference of two rational functions as a rational function.
  2. Express the product or quotient of two rational functions as a rational function.
  3. Calculate the composition of two functions.

Section 0.4

Additional Practice Textbook problems: 0.4 #1, 3, 5, 7, 31, 33, 39

  1. State the definition of the zero of a function.
  2. State the Quadratic Formula.
  3. Use the Quadratic Formula to solve a quadratic equation.
  4. Use the Quadratic Formula to find the zeros of a quadratic function.
  5. Use the Quadratic Formula to find the intersection points of two curves.
  6. Use the Quadratic Formula to solve an equation involving a rational expression.
  7. Factor a polynomial of the form \(x^{2}+bx+c\).
  8. Factor a polynomial of the form \(ax^{2}+bx+c\).
  9. Factor a polynomial of the form \(ax^{3}+bx^{2}+cx\).
  10. Factor a polynomial using one of the 5 special case formulae.

Section 0.5

Additional Practice Textbook problems: 0.5 #33, 35, 39, 41, 43, 45, 89, 91

  1. Identify the different exponential rules.
  2. Simplify an exponential expression using the exponent rules.

Section 1.1

Additional Practice Textbook problems: 1.1 #1, 3, 7, 11, 15, 27, 29

  1. State the slope formula.
  2. State the point-slope formula for the equation of a line.
  3. Determine the slope and \(y\)-intercept of a line given the following:
    • An equation of the form \(y=mx+b\).
    • An equation of the form \(ax+cy=d\).
  4. Sketch an example of a line with positive slope. Similarly for negative slope.
  5. Give an equation that describes a Vertical Line. Similarly for a Horizontal Line.
  6. Calculate the slope of a line given the following information:
    • Its graph or
    • Two points on the line.
  7. Find an equation of the line given the following information:
    • Slope and a point on the line or
    • Two points on the line
  8. Given a line of the form \(y=mx+b\)
    • Find the slope of a parallel line.
    • Find the slope of a perpendicular line.

Section 1.2

Additional Practice Textbook problems: 1.2 #1, 3, 17, 19, 21

  1. Estimate the slope of the curve at designated point given:
    • A graph of the function and a sketch of the tangent line or
    • A graph of the function.

Section 1.3

Additional Practice Textbook problems: 1.3 #3, 5, 7, 9, 11, 15, 17, 19, 25, 33, 35, 71, 73

  1. Differentiate functions of the form \(f(x)=mx+b\).
  2. Differentiate functions of the form \(f(x)=b\).
  3. Differentiate functions of the form \(f(x)=x^{n}\) using the Power Rule.
    • Be able to rewrite root functions \(\sqrt[n]{x}=x^{1/n}\)
    • Be able to rewrite fractions \(1/x^{n}=x^{-n}\)
  4. Use the derivative to find the slope of the curve at a given point.
  5. Use the derivative to find the slope of the curve at a given \(x\)-value.
  6. Use the derivative to find the equation of the tangent line at a given \(x\)-value.

Section 1.4a

Additional Practice Textbook problems: 1.4 #1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 13, 15, 17, 19, 21

  1. Numerical Methods
    • Find the limit of a function given a table of values.
    • Fill in a table of function values to help find the limit of a function.
  2. Graphical Methods
    • Find the limit of a function given its graph.
  3. State the limit laws.
  4. Find the limit of a function using the limit laws.
  5. Find the limit of a polynomial or rational function.
  6. Calculate a limit using the Factor and Cancel Method.

Section 1.4b

Additional Practice Textbook problems: 1.4 #29, 33, 41

  1. State the definition of the derivative of a function \(f(x)\).
  2. Use the limit definition of the derivative to calculate the derivative of:
    • A polynomial of degree 2 or less (multiply out).
    • A rational function (common denominator).
    • A root function (conjugate method).

Section 1.5

Additional Practice Textbook problems: 1.5 #1, 3, 5, 9, 11, 13, 15

  1. Given the graph of a function \(f(x)\):
    • Determine if the function is continuous at a designated number.
    • Determine if the function is differentiable at a designated number.
    • Determine the \(x\)-values where a function is discontinuous.
    • Determine the \(x\)-values where a function is non-differentiable.
  2. State the definition of continuous at a number \(a\).
  3. Determine whether a piecewise function is continuous and/or differentiable at a designated number (not given the graph of the function).

Section 1.6

Additional Practice Textbook problems: 1.6 #1, 5, 9, 11, 13, 17, 19, 29, 41

  1. State the Constant Multiple Rule.
  2. Differentiate functions of the form \(f(x)=kx^{r}\).
  3. State the Sum Rule.
  4. Differentiate various functions using the constant multiple, sum, and power rules.
  5. Differentiate a general power function using the General Power Rule.

Section 1.7

Additional Practice Textbook problems: 1.7 #1, 3, 7, 13, 19

  1. Compute derivatives of functions with variables other than \(x\).
  2. Compute the second derivative of a function (without needing the General Power Rule).
  3. Compute the second derivative of a function (requiring the General Power Rule).
  4. Evaluate the second derivative at a given \(x\)-value.

Section 1.8

Additional Practice Textbook problems: 1.8 #1, 3, 7, 9, 13, 17

  1. Calculate the average rate of change of a function \(f(x)\) over a given interval \([a,b]\).
  2. Compute the instantaneous rate of change of a function \(f(x)\) at a given \(x\)-value.
  3. Given a position function \(s(t)\) that represents the position of an object moving in a straight line:
    • Calculate the average velocity over a given time interval \([a,b]\).
    • Find the velocity function \(v(t)\).
    • Find the acceleration function \(a(t)\).
    • Solve problems involving the position, velocity, and acceleration (determine when the position, velocity, or acceleration is a certain value).
