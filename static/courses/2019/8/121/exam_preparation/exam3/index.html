Exam 3 Study Guide

Logistics

  • Date: Thursday, NovemberÂ 21,Â 2019

  • Time: 8:45pm-10:15pm ET - (it is a 1.5 hour exam)

  • Location: Your exam location is posted on UBlearns.

  • What to Bring: You should bring multiple pens / pencils, your approved calculator, and a photo ID.

    • Calculator: You are allowed to use only the Texas Instruments TI-30XIIS calculator on the exam. All other calculators are prohibited. See the Calculator and Reference Policy in the syllabus for more details.

    • Photo ID: You must bring a photo ID with you to your test site (either a UB student ID or a state-issued ID like a driver's licencse).

  • What Not to Bring: Do not bring any bags, books, notes, formula sheets, or other loose items.

    • No Formula Sheets: You are not allowed any outside references (e.g., cheat sheets, formula sheets, note cards, or textbooks). See the Calculator and Reference Policy in the syllabus for more details.

Material

  • Topics: Exam 3 will explicitly cover material from Ch.4 - Ch.6. Although you will definitely need to know our various derivative rules, like the Product, Quotient, and Chain Rules.

  • Questions: Exam 3 will consist of approximately 15-20 short-answer questions and 3-5 long-answer questions.

Learning Outcomes Being Tested

Below is the list of potential exam questions for each section covered in lecture.

Exams 1 and 2 Review

While concepts from exams 1 and 2 will not be explicitly tested on this exam, we do rely heavily on many of the concepts first introduced there. In particular, it may be a good idea to review:

  • Section 0.4 How To Solve Equations - Factoring and Quadratic Formula
  • Section 1.6 Basic Derivative Rules
  • Section 3.1 Product and Quotient Rules
  • Section 3.2 The Chain Rule

Section 4.1

Additional Practice Textbook problems: 4.1 #17, 19, 21, 23, 25, 29

  1. Sketch the graph of an exponential function of the form \(b^{x}\) or \(b^{-x}\).
  2. Evaluate an exponential function.
  3. Simplify an exponential expression using the exponential laws.
  4. Solve an exponential equation.

Section 4.2

Additional Practice Textbook problems: 4.2 #9, 11, 13, 15, 17, 25, 27, 29, 31, 33, 35, 37, 43, 45

  1. State the slope of \(e^{x}\) at \(x=0\).
  2. State the derivative of \(e^{x}\).
  3. Differentiate functions involving \(e^{x}\).
    • May require the product, quotient, or chain rules.
  4. Sketch the graph of \(e^{kx}\) or \(e^{-kx}\).

Section 4.3

Additional Practice Textbook problems: 4.3 #1, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19, 21, 23, 27, 29, 33

  1. Differentiate functions of the form \(e^{kx}\).
  2. Differentiate functions using the General Exponential Rule.
    • May require the product, quotient, or other chain rules.
  3. Find the equation of the tangent line to a curve at a given \(x\)-value (using the General Exponential Rule to help calculate the derivative).
  4. Find and/or classify the critical numbers of a function (using the General Exponential Rule to help calculate the derivative).

Section 4.4

Additional Practice Textbook problems: 4.4 #7, 9, 11, 15, 19, 21, 23, 27, 29, 33, 37, 45, 47

  1. Sketch the graph of \(\ln x\).
  2. State the properties of the graph of the Natural Logarithm function.
  3. Use the cancellation properties to simplify an expression.
  4. Use the cancellation properties to solve an exponential equation.
  5. Use the cancellation properties to solve a logarithmic equation.
  6. Write an exponential function in the form \(b^{x}\) in the form \(e^{(\ln b)x}\)

Section 4.5

Additional Practice Textbook problems: 4.5 #1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11, 13, 15, 19, 25, 29

  1. State the derivative of \(\ln x\).
  2. Differentiate functions involving \(\ln x\).
    • May require the product, quotient, or chain rules.
  3. Differentiate functions using the General Logarithmic Rule.
    • May require the product, quotient, or other chain rules.

Section 4.6

Additional Practice Textbook problems: 4.6 #1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 23, 27, 31, 33, 35, 43, 45, 49

  1. State the properties of the Natural Logarithm function.
  2. Write an expression as a single logarithm in the form \(\ln(A)\).
  3. Differentiate a natural logarithm function by first simplifying it using the log properties.
  4. Differentiate a function using the technique of logarithmic differentiation.

Section 5.1

Additional Practice Textbook problems: 5.1 #19, 21, 23, 25, 27, 29, 31, 33, 35, 37, 41, 43, 47, 51

  1. State the Exponential Growth Equation.

  2. State the Exponential Decay Equation.

  3. Population Growth

    1. Find the growth constant.
    2. Determine the formula that gives the population at time \(t\).
    3. Find the size of the population at a given time.
    4. Find the rate of growth, \(P'(t)\), of the population at a given time.
    5. Determine how long it will take for a growing population to reach a given size.

  4. Radioactive Decay

    1. Find the decay constant given the half-life.
    2. Determine the formula that gives the radioactive mass remaining at time \(t\).
    3. Find the remaining mass after a given number of years.
    4. Find the rate of decay, \(m'(t)\), of the mass at a given time.
    5. Determine how long it will take for a decaying sample to reach a given mass.

Sections 5.2 and 0.5

Additional Practice Textbook problems: 0.5 #97, 99, 101, 103, 105, 107 --- 5.2 #3, 5, 7, 9, 11, 13, 19, 21

  1. State the formula for compound interest when interest is compounded \(m\) times per year.
  2. Calculate the future value of an investment for different compounding periods.
  3. Calculate the amount of time needed for a deposit to grow to a specified future value for different compounding periods.
  4. State the formula for compound interest when interest is compounded continuously.
  5. Calculate the future value of an investment with continuous compounding.
  6. Calculate the time needed for a deposit to grow to a specified future value with continuous compounding.
  7. Calculate the doubling or tripling time for an investment compounded continuously.

Section 6.1

Additional Practice Textbook problems: 6.1 #1-23(odd)

  1. Calculate the antiderivative of a function using one of the Basic Indefinite Integral Rules. (You may need to rewrite any root functions or fractions.)
    • \(f(x)=k\)
    • \(f(x)=x^{n}\qquad n\neq-1\)
    • \(f(x)=x^{-1}\)
    • \(f(x)=e^{x}\)
    • \(f(x)=e^{kx}\)
  2. Identify the Indefinite Integral Properties.
  3. Calculate the antiderivative of a function requiring the Sum and Constant Multiple Rules.
  4. Calculate the Indefinite Integral of a function that first needs to be simplified by multiplication or division.
  5. Find the position function \(s(t)\) (including the \(+C\)) given the velocity function and the starting position.
  6. Find the value of \(k\) that makes an integral formula true.

Section 6.2

Additional Practice Textbook problems: 6.2 #1-21(odd)

  1. Calculate the Definite Integral of a function involving only the Basic Integration Rules.
  2. Calculate the Definite Integral of a function requiring the Sum and Constant Multiple Rules.
  3. Calculate the Definite Integral of a function that first needs to be simplified by multiplication or division.
  4. Identify the Definite Integral Properties that involve the limits of integration.
  5. Calculate the Definite Integral of a function using the Definite Integral Properties (that involve the limits of integration).
  6. Calculate the Definite Integral of a piecewise-function.
  7. Calculate the Displacement of an object moving in a straight line.
