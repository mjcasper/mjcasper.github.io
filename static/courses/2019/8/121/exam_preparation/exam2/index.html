Exam 2 Study Guide

Logistics

Material

  • Topics: Exam 2 will explicitly cover material from Ch.2 - Ch.3

  • Questions: Exam 2 will consist of approximately 15-20 short-answer questions and 3-5 long-answer questions.

Learning Outcomes Being Tested

Below is the list of potential exam questions for each section covered in lecture.

Exam 1 Review

While concepts from exam 1 will not be explicitly tested on this exam, we do rely heavily on many of the concepts first introduced there. In particular, it may be a good idea to review:

  • Section 0.4 How To Solve Equations - Factoring and Quadratic Formula
  • Section 1.6 Basic Derivative Rules

Section 2.1

Additional Practice 2.1 #1, 3, 17, 25, 27, 29, 33, 35 (textbook)

  1. Given the graph of a function:
    • Find the intervals of increase and decrease.
    • Find the relative extreme points.
    • Find the absolute extreme points.
    • Find the intervals of concavity.
    • Find the inflection points.
  2. Sketch the graph of a function, given the:
    • Intervals of increase and decrease
    • Relative extreme points.
    • Absolute extreme points.
    • Intervals of concavity.
    • Inflection points.

Section 2.2

Additional Practice 2.2 #5, 7, 9, 13, 15, 25, 27 (textbook)

  1. Given the graph of \(f(x)\):
    • Find the intervals where \(f'(x)\) is positive or negative.
    • Find the intervals where \(f''(x)\) is positive or negative.
  2. Given the graph of \(f'(x)\):
    • Find the intervals where \(f(x)\) is increasing or decreasing.
    • Find the intervals where \(f(x)\) is concave up or concave down.
    • Find the \(x\)-values where \(f(x)\) has a relative maximum or minimum
    • Find the \(x\)-values where \(f(x)\) has an inflection point.
  3. Sketch the graph of \(f(x)\), given:
    • The intervals where \(f'(x)\) is positive and negative
    • The \(x\)-values where \(f'(x)=0\)
    • The intervals where \(f''(x)\) is positive and negative
    • The \(x\)-values where \(f';(x)=0\)

Section 2.3

Additional Practice 2.3 #1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 17, 19, 25, 27, 29, 35, 37 (textbook)

  • For all of the following problems, you may be given:
    • the derivatives, \(f'(x)\) or \(f''(x)\), already calculated for you.
    • only the function \(f(x)\), and need to calculate the derivatives for yourself.
  1. Find the critical numbers of a function.
  2. Classify the critical numbers of a function using:
    • the 1st Derivative Test (relative max, relative min, or neither)
    • the 2nd Derivative Test (relative max, relative min, or inconclusive)
  3. Use \(f'(x)\) to find the following about \(f(x)\):
    • Intervals of increase / decrease.
    • Local extreme points.
  4. Use \(f''(x)\) to find the following about \(f(x)\):
    • Intervals of concave up / concave down.
    • Inflection points.
  5. Sketch the graph of a function by determining the:
    • Intervals of increase / decrease.
    • Local extreme points.
    • Intervals of concave up / concave down.
    • Inflection points.

Section 2.4

Additional Practice 2.4 #1, 3, 7, 9, 25, 27 (textbook)

  1. Find the \(x\) and \(y\) intercepts of a function, if any exist.
  2. Sketch the graph of a function by determining the:
    • Intervals of increase / decrease.
    • Local extreme points.
    • Intervals of concave up / concave down.
    • Inflection points.
    • \(x\) and \(y\) intercepts
  3. Sketch the graph of a function of the form \(f(x)=mx+b + \frac{1}{x}\) by determining the:
    • Intervals of increase / decrease.
    • Local extreme points.
    • Intervals of concave up / concave down.
    • Inflection points.
    • Vertical Asymptote at \(x=0\)
    • Oblique (Slant) Asymptote at \(y=mx+b\)

Section 2.5

Additional Practice 2.5 #5, 7, 9, 11, 13, 15, 17, 19, 27 (textbook)

  1. Find the absolute maximum (or minimum) of a function:
    • that has only one critical number.
    • on an interval of the form \([a,\infty)\) or \([a,b]\).
  2. Given a function that describes the motion of a ball thrown straight up into the air:
    • Determine how long it takes to reach its maximum height.
    • Determine the maximum height.
  3. Solve an optimization problem where the objective and constraint equations are given for you.
    • Solve the constraint equation for one of the variables.
    • Substitute this result into the objective equation.
    • Optimize the objective equation (that now only has one variable)
    • Justify your answer by using either the First Derivative or Second Derivative Test to show that there is a maximum / minimum there.
  4. Determine the Objective and Constraint Equations for an optimization word problem.
  5. Solve an optimization word problem by constructing the objective and constraint equations for yourself - and then going through the optimization process as described above. It will involve one of the following:
    • Cost / perimeter and area of a rectangle.
    • Surface Area and Volume of a 3-dimensional box.
    • State the final answer using units, if necessary.

Section 2.6

Additional Practice 2.6 #1, 3, 5, 25 (textbook)

  1. Find the Objective and Constraint Equations for an Inventory Control problem.
  2. Solve an Inventory Control problem using the same Optimization techniques from section 2.5.

Section 3.1

Additional Practice 3.1 #1, 3, 7, 9, 13, 15, 19 23 25 29 31 37 (textbook)

  1. Differentiate a function using the Product Rule.
  2. Differentiate a function using the Quotient Rule.

Section 3.2

Additional Practice 3.2 #11-21(odd), 25, 29, 31, 33, 37, 39, 41, 43, 53, 55 (textbook)

  1. Use the General Power Rule to differentiate \((g(x))^n\).
  2. Use the Chain Rule to differentiate a function of the form \(f(g(x))\).
    • You may be given separate formulas for \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\).
  3. Use the Chain Rule to calculate \(\frac{dy}{dx}\).
    • You may be given separate formulas for \(y=f(u)\) and \(u=g(x)\).
  4. Evaluate a derivative at a given \(x\)-value.
  5. Use the Chain Rule to solve problems involving the rate of change with respect to time. (See 14 and 15 from the homework)

Section 3.3

Additional Practice 3.3 #1, 3, 11, 13, 19, 21, 23, 25, 29, 31, 33, 39, 42, 43, 45 (textbook)

  1. Use implicit differentiation to calculate \(\frac{dy}{dx}\) in terms of \(x\) and \(y\).
    • The differentiation may require use of the Product, Quotient, or General Power Rules.
  2. Use implicit differentiation to find the slope of the curve at a designated point.
  3. Determine the Related Rates Equation for a given equation.
  4. Solve a Related Rates Equation for either \(\frac{dy}{dt}\) or \(\frac{dx}{dt}\)
  5. Evaluate a Related Rates Equation given a specified point and derivative. Use this to solve for the unknown derivative value.
  6. Solve a Related Rates Problem (like 14-17 from the homework)
    • You may need to know the Pythagorean Theoerm.
