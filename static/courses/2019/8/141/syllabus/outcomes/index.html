Learning Outcomes

Unit 1

The following learning outcomes will be assessed in the Homework, Midterm ExamÂ #1, and Final Exam:

  1. Evaluate limits using the definition and using algebraic properties of limits.
  2. Evaluate limits of functions at infinity and interpret them as horizontal asymptotes.
  3. Define continuity and determine whether or not a function is continuous at a point and on an interval.
  4. Define derivative and interpret it as the slope of a tangent to the graph of a function.

Unit 2

The following learning outcomes will be assessed in the Homework, Midterm ExamÂ #2, and Final Exam:

  1. Recognize exponential, logarithmic, and inverse trigonometric functions, sketch their graph and use their basic properties in computations.
  2. Compute derivatives of polynomial, exponential, logarithmic, trigonometric, and inverse trigonometric functions.
  3. Compute derivatives using derivative rules, including the chain rule and implicit differentiation.
  4. Use derivatives to compute linear approximations of functions.

Unit 3

The following learning outcomes will be assessed in the Homework, Midterm ExamÂ #3, and Final Exam:

  1. Find critical points, minima and maxima of a function using its first and second derivatives.
  2. Use derivatives to solve optimization problems.
  3. Use derivatives to sketch graphs of functions.
  4. Apply L'Hospital's rule to compute limits of functions.
  5. Use derivatives to solve practical problems involving rectilinear motion.
  6. Find the area of a region bounded by a curve and the x-axis using rectangles and limits.
  7. Find the area of a region bounded by a curve and the x-axis using indefinite integrals and the fundamental theorem of calculus.
  8. Use integrals to solve practical problems involving rectilinear motion.

Unit 4

The following learning outcomes will be assessed in the Homework, and Final Exam:

  1. Define the limit of a function at a point.
  2. State the mean value theorem and apply it in computations.

Meta

The following learning outcomes will be assessed in all Homework, Midterm ExamsÂ #1-3, and the Final Exam:

  1. Choose appropriate methods or models for a given problem, using information from observed or deduced data and knowledge of the system being studied.
  2. Employ quantitative methods, mathematical models, statistics, and/or logic to solve real-world problems beyond the level of basic algebra.
  3. Identify common mistakes and/or limitations in empirical and deductive reasoning, and in mathematical, quantitative, and/or logical problem solving.
  4. Interpret mathematical models, formulas, graphs, and/or tables, to draw inferences from them, and explain these inferences.
